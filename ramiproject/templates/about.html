About Nefashot

מיזם נפשות פועל למען העלאת המודעות לתחום בריאות הנפש במרחב הציבורי באמצעות אירועי תרבות אמנות ושיח. מיזם נפשות מקדם עשייה משמעותית לאורך כל השנה, על מנת לשנות את השיח הקיים ולהעלות למודעות הציבורית את החשיבות בעיסוק בנושא. הן עבור האנשים החווים מצוקות נפשיות, הן עבור הסובבים אותם (בני משפחה וחברים), והן עבור כל אחת ואחד מאיתנו - כדי שנלמד להיות אמפתיים, לשתף ולפנות לקבל עזרה בעת משבר בלי בושה מהסטיגמה.

Nefashot (Souls in Hebrew) is a social initiative whose purpose is to raise awareness about the field of mental health in the public space through art, culture, and dialogue-based events.

Nefashot Mental Health Festival started as a Jerusalem initiative and in the first two years, the events mainly took place in Jerusalem’s public space. However, in 2020, due to the social distancing restrictions surrounding the corona pandemic, the activity expanded geographically as the events were mostly virtual. Such an unexpected reach led to nationwide involvement, and today, the Nefashot Mental Health Festival will take place in the north, the south, the center, and of course in Jerusalem.

Beyond Nefashot’s Mental Health Week, Nefashot actively promotes and participates in mental health activism throughout the year in order to change the existing conversation and raise public awareness about this issue. Our work seeks to open new spaces for dialogue involving people experiencing mental distress, their support systems, and society at large. These spaces give us, on the one hand, the opportunity to listen, understand, and expand our preconceived notions about mental health and on the other hand, the chance to personally de-stigmatize mental distress and seek help in a crisis without shame.

We believe that human encounters involving art, creativity, and dialogue are a powerful vehicle towards an authentic and much-needed understanding of mental health.

Mental health is a human issue that involves all of us. We believe that raising awareness about mental health is our duty. We feel a sense of responsibility towards the communities in which we live and the individuals that make up these communities.
